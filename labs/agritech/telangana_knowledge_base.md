## Regional climate envelope (Telangana — Suryapet / Jangaon / Nalgonda triangle)

### Common features (all three districts)
- **Climate type**: Semi-arid, tropical
- **Summer (Mar-Jun)**: 37-41°C peak; minimum 22-26°C
- **Winter (Nov-Feb)**: maximum 24-28°C; minimum 13-18°C
- **Monsoon (Jun-Sep)**: SW monsoon dominant; ~70% of annual rainfall
- **Frost risk**: None (no frost-sensitive constraint)

### Per-district differences

| Dimension | Suryapet | Jangaon | Nalgonda |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual rainfall | ~731 mm | ~680 mm (drier) | ~750-800 mm (wetter, northern higher) |
| Summer peak | 38-40°C | 39-41°C (hottest of three) | 37-40°C |
| Dominant soil | Red soil + chalka sub-type, some black cotton patches | Black cotton (Regur) dominant; red on outskirts | Mixed: alluvial near Krishna river / red + chalka central / black cotton scattered |
| Major markets | Suryapet local · Hyderabad Monda Market (120 km) · Warangal (90 km) | Jangaon local · **Warangal Wholesale (60 km — primary)** · Hyderabad (150 km) | Nalgonda local · Miryalaguda mandi · Hyderabad Monda (100 km) |
| KVK | Suryapet KVK | Warangal KVK (serves Jangaon) | Nalgonda KVK |
| Historical staple crops | Cotton, paddy, chillies, pulses, mango | Paddy, cotton, chillies, maize | Paddy, cotton, Bengal gram, redgram, chillies, sweet lime (Mosambi) |
| Water sources | Bore wells dominant; some Krishna lift irrigation | Bore wells; limited Manair river access | Krishna river (alluvial belt) · Nagarjuna Sagar canal command area · bore wells |
| Wildlife pressure | Forest-adjacent villages: monkey + wild boar; Nallamala forest edge | Lower forest proximity; less wildlife pressure overall; Pakhal sanctuary edge | Variable — Nallamala forest fringe (south) high pressure; canal belt low |
| Specialty crops | Mango (Banganapalli) — regional reputation; cotton | Chilli (Guntur-area influence); maize | Sweet lime (Mosambi) — Tirumalagiri famous; Bengal gram |

### Implications for crop fit

- **Hass avocado FAILS in all three** (summer crosses 35°C — recommend Fuerte, Pollock, Ettinger, Pinkerton — green-skin varieties)
- **Paddy viable** in canal command (Nalgonda) and bore-well-rich pockets; risky rainfed
- **Drought-tolerant crops** (ragi, jowar, bajra, pulses, sesame, horsegram) viable rainfed in all three
- **Perennials** require drip irrigation for commercial success; subsidized 80-100% under MIDH
- **Black cotton patches** (especially Jangaon) need raised beds for perennials to prevent waterlogging root rot
- **Cotton viable** in all three; Jangaon's black soil is the strongest match

## Soil typology (Telangana detail)

### Red soil (most common in Suryapet, parts of Jangaon, central Nalgonda)
- **Chalka** sub-type: sandy red soil, lighter, well-drained, lower fertility
- **Red loam** sub-type: more clay content, better water-holding
- **pH**: 6.0-7.5 typical
- **Best for**: groundnut, millets, pulses, lemon, mango, drumstick, dragon fruit
- **Constraint**: Lower water retention than black; needs frequent irrigation for water-loving crops

### Black cotton / Regur (dominant in Jangaon, patches in Suryapet + Nalgonda)
- **pH**: 7.5-8.5 alkaline tendency
- **Best for**: cotton, sorghum, soybean, pigeonpea, sunflower, Bengal gram, wheat (irrigated)
- **Constraint**: Heavy clay; waterlogging risk in monsoon — PERENNIALS NEED RAISED BEDS
- **Cracks** in dry season aid root penetration but harm shallow crops

### Alluvial (Krishna river belt of Nalgonda)
- Newer (khadar) near banks; older (bhangar) further out
- High fertility, good for paddy + sugarcane + vegetables + banana
- pH 6.5-7.5
- Best soil among the three districts; underutilized in Telangana relative to UP/Bihar/Bengal

### Saline / alkaline patches (rare but present in older canal command areas)
- Salt-tolerant crops: barley, mustard, dhaincha (green manure), date palm, mesquite

## Variety-level crop economics for Telangana

### LEMON (perennial, 3-year breakeven, 8-year peak)

| Variety | Premium vs Kagzi | Peak ₹/kg | Year 8+ net ₹/acre | Suryapet/Jangaon/Nalgonda fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Kagzi Lime (standard) | baseline | ₹30-45 | ₹40-60K | All three good |
| **Thailand Lemon** | **+40-50%** | ₹45-65 | **₹50K-1L** ★ | All three — recommended primary |
| Vikram Seedless | +60-80% | ₹55-75 | ₹70K-1.3L | All three — premium retail/export |
| Pramalini (PKM-1) | +25-35% | ₹40-55 | ₹45-70K | Disease-resistant backup |
| Balaji | +15-20% | ₹35-50 | ₹40-65K | Wholesale + processing focus |

**Telangana structural advantage**: state deficit ~30-40K tons/year → ₹5-15/kg structural premium vs surplus AP.

**Seasonal price windows** (farm gate):
- Peak: April-May (summer heat → demand spike), November (Diwali)
- Trough: July-August (monsoon glut)
- Stable: rest of year

**Suppliers**: SKLTSHU Rajendranagar (govt); Deccan Exotics (Hyderabad, Plot 223 Vivekananda Colony Kukatpally); ICAR-CISH (Lucknow) for Pramalini cuttings.

### AVOCADO (perennial, 3-4 year breakeven, Year 5+ peak)

**Hass DOES NOT WORK** in Telangana (>35°C summer). Recommend:

| Variety | Type | Heat tolerance | Suryapet fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fuerte** | Green-skin | High | ★ Primary recommendation |
| Pollock | Green-skin | High (Indian heritage variety since 1941) | ★ |
| Ettinger | Green-skin | High (commercial Israeli) | ★ |
| Pinkerton | Green-skin | High | ★ |
| Hass | Black-skin | Low | ❌ AVOID |

**Rootstock**: Ashdot 17 (Israeli, Phytophthora-resistant) — non-negotiable for commercial planting. Avoid cheap random-seedling grafts.

**Spacing**:
- Standard 6×6m (110-115 trees/acre)
- **Intensive 3.5×7m (165-170 trees/acre) — recommended**
- High-density 500-800 trees/ha

**Economics (1 acre, mature)**:
- Year 1 investment: ₹1.5-2.3 lakh (drip + plants + prep)
- Mature yield: ~7,500 kg/acre (250g avg fruit × 30,000 fruits)
- Wholesale ₹150/kg current → ₹11.25 lakh gross
- Net Year 5+: ₹10-13 lakh/acre

**Critical risks**:
1. **Phytophthora root rot** — kills established trees, no cure. Prevention via raised beds (esp. black soil), well-drained sites, controlled drip, resistant rootstock.
2. **Hot dry winds** — windbreaks (Subabul, Casuarina) on western/southern edges.
3. **Anthracnose** post-harvest in humid pockets — copper sprays from fruit set to harvest.

**Suppliers**: Indo Israel Avocado (Ashdot 17 rootstock); Deccan Exotics (40-acre nursery in Sangareddy); SKLTSHU.

**Recommended pilot**: 0.5-1 acre before scaling, per published Telangana Hort Dept guidance.

### DRAGON FRUIT (perennial, 18-24 month breakeven — fast)

**Suryapet's emerging high-margin crop. Very low water + zero wildlife pressure (cactus).**

| Variety | Type | Color | Premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Red-flesh Vietnamese | Hylocereus polyrhizus | Magenta | High |
| Red-flesh Israeli | improved varieties | Magenta | Highest |
| White-flesh | Hylocereus undatus | White | Standard |
| Yellow-skin | Selenicereus megalanthus | Yellow | Niche premium |

**Economics**:
- Spacing: 3m × 3m (~450 plants/acre); concrete pole + cactus growing on it
- Year 1 investment: ₹3-5 lakh/acre (poles + plants + drip)
- First fruit: 18-24 months
- Peak production: Year 4+; 8-10 tons/acre
- Wholesale: ₹150-300/kg (Hyderabad retail ₹200-400/kg)
- **Net at peak: ₹3-5 lakh/acre**

**Why Suryapet fit**: tolerates 38-42°C; minimal water (~250mm rainfall-equivalent); negligible pest pressure; **monkeys + wild boar avoid cactus**; **MIDH 50% subsidy on planting material**.

**Suppliers**: Krishi Nursery (Hyderabad); SKLTSHU; Indo Israel Agriculture.

### POMEGRANATE (perennial, 2-3 year breakeven)

| Variety | Trait |
|---|---|
| **Bhagwa** | Export-grade; Maharashtra/AP/Karnataka strong; high premium |
| Ganesh | Older variety, lower yield |

### COTTON (annual, 6-month cycle)

Jangaon's strongest match (black cotton soil). Bt cotton dominant. Bollworm + pink bollworm management critical. Net ₹/acre: highly variable ₹30K-1 lakh depending on yield + price; **price volatility is the killer**.

## Exotic / high-value crops (the premium-niche play)

### Aloe Vera
- **Extremely drought-tolerant**, minimal input
- First harvest 12-14 months, multi-year crop
- Sale to cosmetic + medicinal industry; processing units in Hyderabad, Bangalore
- **Net ₹50K-1L/acre/year** at maturity
- Wildlife pressure: zero (bitter)
- **Best for marginal/degraded land** where other crops fail

### Stevia
- Sugar substitute; growing health market
- 6 months to first harvest; multiple harvests/year
- ₹1-2 lakh/acre net at peak
- Requires partial shade in peak summer; intercrop with taller crops

### Lemongrass
- Essential oil extraction; aromatic
- 5+ year perennial; first harvest at 6 months
- ₹60K-1.2 lakh/acre net
- **Native distillation units** in AP/Telangana; Suryapet/Nalgonda viable

### Passion Fruit
- Yellow + Purple varieties
- Trellis system needed; 18-month to bearing
- ₹1.5-2.5 lakh/acre net; premium juice/pulp market
- Wildlife pressure: low

### Fig (Anjeer)
- Poona / Diana / Brown Turkey varieties
- 2 years to bearing; semi-arid native
- ₹1.5-3 lakh/acre net at maturity
- Fresh + dried markets (dried fetches 3x)
- Bird pressure: moderate (netting helps)

### Dates (Khajur)
- Barhi (early-bearing semi-dry) / Medjool (premium soft)
- **Extremely drought-tolerant** (250-400mm)
- 5-7 years to bearing; multi-decade asset
- ₹1.5-3 lakh/acre net at maturity; Gujarat/Rajasthan model
- Telangana experimental — early adopters can corner regional supply

### Moringa (commercial drumstick)
- PKM-1 / Bhagya / Coimbatore-2 varieties
- 6 months to first harvest
- Triple revenue: fresh pods (₹40K/acre), leaves powder export (₹50K-1L/acre), seeds (₹20K/acre)
- **Zero wildlife pressure**; very low water; rainfed-feasible
- Boundary crop OR primary crop at scale

### Olive
- Arbequina / Koroneiki varieties
- 3-5 years to bearing; experimental in Telangana
- Rajasthan + MP pilots; cooking oil market
- ₹1-2 lakh/acre net (variable, experimental status)

**Per cow economics**:
- Daily milk × ₹40-60/L = ₹400-1200 daily revenue
- Feed + vet + labor: ₹250-500/day
- **Net ₹150-700/cow/day = ₹4.5-21K/cow/month**
- Manure: 20-25 kg/day → free fertilizer for ~1 acre
- Calving: ~1 calf/year (additional asset)

**Govt schemes**: NLM (National Livestock Mission, 25-50% on indigenous breeds), RKVY, NDDB linkages for milk procurement (Vijaya, Heritage, Mother Dairy).

### APIARY (BEE KEEPING)

| Species | Behavior | Yield/box | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Apis cerana indica** (native) | Smaller hive, hardy, defensive | 8-15 kg/year | Recommended for beginners; native pollinators |
| **Apis mellifera** (Italian) | Larger hive, gentler, higher yield | 20-30 kg/year | Requires more management; commercial scale |
| Trigona (stingless) | Very small, niche | 1-3 kg/year | Medicinal honey premium |

**Economics**:
- Box cost: ₹3-5K each (with bee colony)
- Annual maintenance: ₹500-1000/box
- Honey wholesale: ₹150-300/kg
- **Per box revenue (mellifera): 25 kg × ₹250 = ₹6,250/year**
- **MIDH subsidy: ₹2,000/box (50%) up to 20 boxes**

**Pollination benefit to neighboring crops** (often overlooked):
- Mango, lemon, avocado, drumstick: 20-40% yield boost
- Tomato, mustard, sunflower, sesame: 25-50% yield boost
- Pomegranate, dragon fruit: significant benefit

**Placement**: Within 100-200m of flowering crops; relocate seasonally.

**Suppliers + training**: KVK apiculture programs; Mohan Bee Keeping (Pune); KVIC nodal officers.

### POULTRY

- **Backyard / desi (Aseel, Kadaknath, Vanaraja)**: 50-150 birds, low input, ₹15-30K revenue/year, manure for crops
- **Commercial layer**: 500+ hens, ₹3-5L investment, ₹50K-1L net/year, high disease risk
- **Commercial broiler**: 4-5 cycles/year, ₹1-2L net/cycle at scale
- **Telangana Aseel** known for fighting cock genetics + premium meat market

### FISH (POND AQUACULTURE)

- **Indian Major Carps (rohu, catla, mrigal)**: ₹40-50K pond setup, ₹80K-1.5L revenue/year
- **Pangasius (intensive)**: higher yield, ₹2-3L net possible but more management
- **Tilapia**: profitable in Telangana climate
- Nalgonda canal belt has tank infrastructure — fish farming subsidy via Fisheries Dept

### SERICULTURE (silk farming)

- Mulberry-based; AP has strongest support; Telangana developing
- ₹40-60K/acre net at maturity; cocoon prices govt-regulated floor
- Labor-intensive (cocoon harvest every 25-30 days)
- Department of Sericulture training + nursery support

### MUSHROOM

- **Oyster mushroom** (Pleurotus ostreatus): low investment ₹20-30K, 30-day cycle, urban demand
- **Milky mushroom** (Calocybe indica): tropical-friendly, higher temp tolerance
- **Button mushroom**: needs controlled-environment, higher investment ₹2-5L

## SUSTAINABILITY PRACTICES

### Zero-Budget Natural Farming (ZBNF / Natural Farming)

Subhash Palekar's framework. Strong govt support in AP (Rythu Sadhikara Samstha — RySS); Telangana growing programs.

**4 wheels**:
1. **Jeevamruta** — microbial culture (cow dung + urine + jaggery + pulse flour + soil)
2. **Bijamruta** — seed treatment with same microbial mix
3. **Achhadana (mulching)** — straw, leaves, intercrop residues
4. **Whaphasa (moisture)** — soil moisture conservation via mulching + minimal tillage

**Economics**: 70-80% input cost reduction; yields stabilize at 80-90% of conventional after 2-3 transition years.

**Certification path**: PGS-India (Participatory Guarantee System) for community-scale; NPOP for export-grade. Organic certification takes 3 years.

### Agroforestry

- **Subabul + cotton**: Subabul (Leucaena) fixes N, provides fuelwood + fodder
- **Drumstick + ragi**: Boundary perennial + drought-tolerant cereal
- **Mango + pulses**: 5-year window before mango canopy closes
- **Coconut + banana + black pepper**: 3-tier in Kerala; less common Telangana
- **Casuarina + crops**: Windbreak + 5-year pulp wood harvest

**Carbon credit potential**: Verra (VCS), NABARD pilots, India Carbon Market (in development).

### Rainwater harvesting

- **Farm pond** (3-5m × 3-5m × 2m): NREGA-eligible (free labor for sanctioned schemes)
- **Check dams**: community-scale; Telangana Mission Kakatiya program
- **Rooftop harvesting**: limited but possible

**Storage**: ~1 acre-foot per 1000 sqm catchment per heavy rain event.

### Solar pumps

- **PM-KUSUM scheme**: 60-90% subsidy; individual farmer + community installations
- Replaces diesel pumps; OPEX → zero after install
- Surplus solar can sell to grid in some states (not yet Telangana)

### Biogas

- 2-4 cattle = enough manure for 1 cum biogas plant
- Plant cost: ₹35-40K; MNRE subsidy 50-90%
- Output: cooking gas (replaces 2-3 LPG cylinders/month) + slurry (high-quality compost)

### Composting + vermicomposting

- Vermicompost (Eisenia foetida earthworms): ₹500-1000/quintal sale price; on-farm production
- Cattle manure compost: free fertilizer; key ZBNF input

### Drip irrigation

- **MIDH subsidy: 80-100%** depending on category (SC/ST/marginal/small farmer get 100%)
- 30-50% water savings vs flood
- Critical for perennials in Telangana

### Mulching

- Straw, paddy husk, dry leaves
- 50-70% reduction in evaporation
- Free input from on-farm residues

### Mixed / intercrop / rotation patterns (Telangana-specific)

| Pattern | Region | Logic |
|---|---|---|
| Pigeonpea + sorghum | Deccan | Tall + short, different roots, pigeonpea N-fixing |
| Maize + cowpea | All three districts | Pulse fixes N for cereal |
| Cotton + greengram | Jangaon | Greengram harvested before cotton peaks |
| Sugarcane + onion | Canal command | Onion in inter-rows, short cycle |
| Drumstick + ragi | All three | Boundary perennial + drought crop |
| Lemon + greengram | Years 1-2 of lemon plantation | Cover crop while tree establishes |
| Avocado + pulse cover | Years 1-3 of avocado | Pulses prevent weeds, fix N |
| Pearl millet + cluster bean | Dry pockets | Both drought-tolerant |

## SUSTAINABILITY SCORING RUBRIC

Composite score (0-100) across 5 axes, weighted equal:

1. **Soil health (0-20)**
   - +5: Crop rotation in plan
   - +5: Cover crops / intercropping
   - +5: Composting / vermicomposting / FYM (>50% of fertilizer)
   - +5: No-till or minimum-till practices

2. **Water efficiency (0-20)**
   - +8: Drip irrigation (all perennials)
   - +5: Rainwater harvesting (farm pond)
   - +4: Mulching practices
   - +3: Drought-tolerant variety choices

3. **Biodiversity (0-20)**
   - +6: ≥3 different crop families in plan
   - +5: Perennials present (carbon + biodiversity)
   - +4: Apiary (pollinator support)
   - +3: Boundary plantings / hedgerows / agroforestry
   - +2: Mixed livestock + crop integration

4. **Carbon balance (0-20)**
   - +8: Solar pump / biogas (replaces fossil energy)
   - +5: Agroforestry / perennials carbon-sequestering
   - +4: No / minimal synthetic fertilizer
   - +3: Composting (closes nutrient loops)

5. **Input self-sufficiency (0-20)**
   - +6: On-farm compost/FYM > 50% of fertilizer
   - +5: ZBNF / organic practices
   - +5: Indigenous breeds (resilience)
   - +4: Native seed varieties (vs hybrid dependency)

**Interpretation**:
- 80-100: Strong regenerative practice; certified-organic pathway viable
- 60-79: Good baseline; clear improvement opportunities
- 40-59: Conventional with some sustainability; substantial room to grow
- <40: Heavy chemical/input dependence; high opportunity for transition

## WILDLIFE PRESSURE MATRIX

| Wildlife | Target crops | Mitigation | Resilient crops to recommend instead |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Monkeys** | Maize, fruits (mango/lemon young), tomato, peanuts | Netting, scarecrow, dogs; difficult to fully prevent | Ragi, drumstick, pulses, sesame, dragon fruit, aloe vera |
| **Wild boar** | Tubers, sugarcane, melons, peanuts | Electric fencing, dogs, community watch | Millets, drumstick, lemon (mature), dragon fruit, perennials |
| **Peacocks** | Tomato young plants, chilli seedlings, ragi shoots | Netting first 30-60 days, tall barriers | Mature perennials (peacocks ground-feed), pulses after establishment |
| **Nilgai** | Wheat, vegetables, pulses | Solar fencing, drumstick hedges | Drumstick, custard apple, citrus |
| **Elephants** | Sugarcane, paddy, banana, jackfruit | Community deterrence, chilli-smoke fences | Avoid these in elephant corridors; chilli + lemon less attractive |
| **Birds** (parrots, mynas) | Sunflower, sorghum, ragi, fig, grapes | Netting, reflective tape | Cereals netted; perennials with thick skin (citrus, custard apple) |

**Forest-adjacent farms** (<2 km from reserve forest): treat wildlife as PRIMARY constraint, not edge case. Recommend wildlife-resilient mix; perennials only where mature plants resist (drumstick, citrus, dragon fruit).

## CONFIDENCE CALIBRATION FOR THE ADVISOR

When generating plans, the advisor should:
- Set **high confidence (>0.85)** for: variety recommendations matching this knowledge base, MIDH subsidy claims, climate-fit verdicts
- Set **medium confidence (0.65-0.85)** for: specific ₹ revenue projections (markets vary year to year), specific yield projections (depend on management), intercrop pattern viability
- Set **low confidence (<0.65)** for: experimental crops (olive, dates, quinoa in Telangana), claims about hyper-local soil conditions, claims about specific govt scheme application timelines, predictions beyond Year 5
- Always escalate to **human review (KVK / agricultural officer / horticulture consultant)** for: large investments (>₹5L), commercial perennial plantations, organic certification path, livestock business plans

**The advisor is decision-support, NOT decision-making.** Every plan ends with: *"Validate with your local KVK officer or agricultural extension service before committing investment. Crop and livestock outcomes depend on management, weather, market conditions, and policy changes."*
